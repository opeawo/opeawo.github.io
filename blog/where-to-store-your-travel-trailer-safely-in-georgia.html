Where to Store Your Travel Trailer Safely in Georgia

Travel trailers need a home when you're not using them, and "safe" means different things to different owners. Some prioritize security against theft. Others worry about weather protection. Many just need a legal place to park that doesn't upset the neighbors. Here's how to evaluate your options.

Understanding Your Storage Needs

Security Concerns

Theft risk factors:

  • Trailer value (newer/nicer = higher target)
  • Location visibility (visible from road = more risk)
  • Neighborhood crime rates
  • How long trailer sits unused

Damage risk factors:

  • Weather exposure (Georgia sun, hail, storms)
  • Vandalism potential
  • Tree coverage (falling branches, sap)

Frequency of Use

Regular use (2+ times/month):

  • Easy access matters
  • Security features still important
  • Location convenience is priority

Seasonal use (a few times/year):

  • Security and protection are primary
  • Can compromise on location convenience
  • Long-term storage considerations apply

Infrequent use (once/year or less):

  • Storage becomes primary purpose
  • Maximum protection appropriate
  • Consider whether ownership still makes sense

Trailer Size

Travel trailers range from 12' to 35'+. Size affects:

  • Where it can legally park
  • What facilities can accommodate it
  • Maneuvering requirements
  • Pricing (larger = more expensive)

Storage Options in Georgia

Home Storage

If possible: The most convenient and cost-effective option.

Requirements:

  • Space (driveway, side yard, backyard)
  • Zoning/HOA permission
  • Safe access (can you maneuver it in/out?)

Advantages:

  • Free
  • Maximum convenience
  • Easy monitoring

Disadvantages:

  • May not be allowed (HOA, zoning)
  • Security depends on your property
  • Takes up space you might use otherwise
  • Neighborhood appearance issues

Storage Facilities

Dedicated vehicle storage:

  • Designed for RVs and trailers
  • Security features
  • Various options (outdoor, covered, enclosed)

Advantages:

  • Professional security
  • No home appearance issues
  • Often better weather protection

Disadvantages:

  • Monthly cost ($75-200+)
  • Less convenient than home
  • Requires trip to access trailer

Private Property Arrangements

Renting space from individuals:

  • Farms, large properties, commercial lots

Advantages:

  • Often cheaper than commercial facilities
  • May be more flexible
  • Sometimes better locations available

Disadvantages:

  • Variable security
  • Less formal arrangements
  • May not be reliable long-term

Security Considerations

Facility Security Features

Basic (minimum acceptable):

  • Complete perimeter fencing
  • Gated entry
  • Locked after hours

Better:

  • Individual access codes
  • Lighting throughout
  • Regular management presence

Best:

  • Camera surveillance
  • On-site staff
  • 24-hour monitoring

Your Security Measures

Regardless of where you store, add your own protection:

Essential:

  • Hitch lock (prevents hooking up and towing)
  • Wheel lock or boot (prevents rolling away)
  • Lock all access points

Additional:

  • GPS tracker (assists recovery if stolen)
  • Alarm system
  • VIN etching

Insurance:

  • Verify your policy covers theft while stored
  • Understand the claim process
  • Document contents and condition

Weather Protection in Georgia

Outdoor Storage

Exposure to:

  • Georgia sun (UV degrades roof, seals, tires)
  • Summer heat (interior temps can hit 150Â°F+)
  • Hail (spring/summer storms)
  • Heavy rain
  • Occasional ice/snow

Mitigation:

  • Quality cover that fits properly
  • Tire covers
  • Regular inspection and maintenance

Covered Storage

Protects from:

  • Direct sun/UV
  • Hail
  • Direct rain
  • Some debris

Doesn't protect from:

  • Heat (still hot under cover)
  • Humidity
  • Blowing rain

Cost: 25-50% more than outdoor

Enclosed Storage

Maximum protection from:

  • All weather
  • UV
  • Some temperature moderation

Cost: 50-100%+ more than outdoor

Availability: Limited for larger trailers

Regional Options

Metro Atlanta

Inside I-285: Very limited, expensive when available

Northern suburbs: Moderate availability, higher pricing

Eastern suburbs (I-20 corridor): Good availability, competitive pricing

Southern suburbs: Variable availability

Rural Georgia

Advantages:

  • More space
  • Lower prices
  • Easier zoning

Disadvantages:

  • Farther from home (usually)
  • Variable security
  • May be harder to find quality facilities

Choosing the Right Option

Decision Framework

  1. Can you store at home legally and practically?
  • If yes, that's usually the best option
  • If no, evaluate facilities
  1. How important is security?
  • High-value trailer or high-crime area = prioritize security
  • Basic trailer, low-crime = basic security may suffice
  1. How important is weather protection?
  • New/valuable trailer = covered or enclosed worth considering
  • Older trailer you'll replace = outdoor is fine
  1. What's your budget?
  • Match facility type to what you can afford
  1. How often will you use it?
  • Frequent use = prioritize convenient location
  • Infrequent use = prioritize protection and cost
