Top Features to Look for in an Atlanta Camper Storage Facility

Not all camper storage facilities are equal, but not all features matter equally either. Some are essential; some are nice-to-have; some are marketing fluff. Knowing the difference helps you evaluate facilities efficiently and find storage that actually serves your needs.

Essential Features (Non-Negotiable)

Adequate Space for Your Camper

The basics:

  • Space length exceeds your camper + tongue + working room
  • Width allows door opening
  • Height clearance (for covered storage) exceeds your camper's height

Why it's essential: You literally can't use storage that doesn't fit your camper.

How to verify: Get your camper's exact dimensions. Ask about specific space sizes. Visit and measure if possible.

Security Fundamentals

The basics:

  • Perimeter fencing (complete, in good repair)
  • Gated access (functional, closes properly)
  • Individual access codes (not shared)

Why it's essential: Your camper represents $10,000-100,000+. Minimum security protects that investment.

How to verify: Visit the facility. Test the gate. Ask about access control.

Maneuvering Space

The basics:

  • Drive aisles wide enough for your tow vehicle and camper
  • Turning radius at row ends
  • Ability to back in without hitting adjacent vehicles

Why it's essential: If you can't get in and out, you can't use the facility.

How to verify: Visit with your tow vehicle (ideally with camper). Drive the lot. Try the maneuvers.

Access Hours That Work

The basics:

  • Hours that match when you'll actually use the camper
  • Early morning access if you like early starts
  • Weekend access for typical camping trips

Why it's essential: A facility you can't access when you need to is useless.

How to verify: Ask specifically about hours. Get written confirmation if unusual hours matter to you.

Important Features (Strongly Recommended)

Lighting

What matters:

  • Coverage throughout the lot
  • Working fixtures
  • Adequate for evening/nighttime visits

Why it matters: Safety for you, deterrent for criminals, visibility for cameras.

How to verify: Visit in evening if possible. Look for dark areas.

Surface Quality

What matters:

  • Stable surface (gravel or pavement)
  • Good drainage (no standing water)
  • Level or nearly level parking

Why it matters: Poor surfaces damage trailers, create stuck situations, and indicate poor management.

How to verify: Visit after rain if possible. Look for ruts, soft spots, standing water.

Camera Coverage

What matters:

  • Cameras at gate and main areas
  • Actually recording (not just for show)
  • Reasonable retention period

Why it matters: Provides evidence if something happens, deters some crime.

How to verify: Ask where cameras are, whether they record, how long footage is kept.

Covered Storage Option

What matters:

  • Roof overhead blocking UV and hail
  • Adequate height for your camper
  • Reasonable premium over outdoor

Why it matters: Georgia sun and hail are hard on campers. Covered storage extends life.

How to verify: Ask about covered availability, pricing, and height clearance.

Nice-to-Have Features (Worth Considering)

Dump Station Access

What it provides: Ability to dump holding tanks on-site before storage

Who needs it: Campers who want convenience of not stopping elsewhere

Reality: Many facilities don't offer this. If important to you, specifically ask.

Water Fill

What it provides: Fill fresh tank before heading out

Who needs it: Convenience-focused campers

Reality: Not common at storage-only facilities.

Electrical Hookups

What it provides: Ability to run battery maintainer, pre-cool AC

Who needs it: Owners who want battery maintenance without visiting

Reality: Usually extra cost if available.

Wash Area

What it provides: Clean your camper on-site

Who needs it: Owners without good washing options at home

Reality: Less common, may have restrictions.

On-Site Propane

What it provides: Fill propane tanks before trips

Who needs it: Convenience seekers

Reality: Rare at most storage facilities.

Features That Are Oversold

"24-Hour Security"

What it usually means: Cameras are recording. Maybe someone reviews footage if you report a problem.

What it doesn't mean: Armed guards, active monitoring, instant response.

Reality check: Adequate basic security (fence, gate, lights) is what most facilities actually provide.

"Climate-Controlled" (For Campers)

What it would mean: Temperature and humidity-managed space.

Reality: Almost never available for camper-sized spaces. Usually marketing confusion with indoor storage.

What you actually need: Covered storage addresses the main concerns at reasonable cost.

Premium Location Claims

What's claimed: "Best location in Atlanta"

What matters: Is the location convenient for YOU? General "best" claims are meaningless.

How to Evaluate Features

Before Visiting

  1. Confirm they have space for your camper size
  2. Ask about security features
  3. Ask about access hours
  4. Get pricing for options you want

During Visit

  1. Observe fence, gate, lighting condition
  2. Drive the lot to assess maneuvering
  3. Check surface quality
  4. See the actual space you'd rent
  5. Trust your overall impression

Questions to Ask

  1. "What's your largest/smallest camper space?"
  2. "How does gate access work?"
  3. "Do you have covered storage available for my size?"
  4. "What's included vs. extra?"
  5. "Has the facility had break-ins? Flooding?"
